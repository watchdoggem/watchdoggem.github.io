Mission Statement

Tracking not just what they spend, but what they ALLOW.

The Core Thesis

Historically, government oversight focused on the flow of tax dollars (The Budget). However, the true cost of governance is often found in the "Allowances"—regulatory loopholes, mandates, and "GRAS" (Generally Recognized As Safe) exemptions that permit industrial harm under the guise of safety.

This tool tracks two distinct economies:

  • The Budget: Where tax dollars go (Defense, Health, etc.).
  • The Allowances: Permitted regulatory harm (e.g., NHP testing, food additives, industrial farming).

2. Regulatory "Hammers" (What is Allowed)

Food Supply (GRAS Loophole)

FDA "GRAS" (Generally Recognized As Safe): Allows companies to self-certify additives like Hexane-extracted oils and Sodium Benzoate without independent safety studies.

Standard of Identity (21 CFR 131): Allows Polysorbate and Calcium Chloride in dairy while still labeling it "Milk."

Public Health Mandates

Fluoride: Active addition of compounds to public water supplies (Code 550 spending) vs. simple filtration.

The "Kitchen Killer" (21 CFR 114): "Acidified Food" regs that impose high "Process Authority" costs, banning small local kitchens while allowing large corporate production.
